The spatial arrangement of vessels determines sensor coverage, weapons arcs, communications redundancy, and the ability to concentrate or disperse force. A poorly arranged formation creates blind spots that adversaries will exploit.

![Fleet formation overview](https://picsum.photos/id/669/1200/400){.rounded-lg width="1200" height="400"}

## The Five Standard Formations

Modern fleet doctrine recognizes five standard formations, each optimizing for a different operational requirement.

::tabs
  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-minus" label="Line"}
  Maximizes transit efficiency — vessels follow a single track, minimizing navigational complexity and fuel expenditure.

  | Attribute | Rating |
  |-----------|--------|
  | Sensor coverage | Low — forward arc only |
  | Offensive power | Low — only lead vessel has clear arcs |
  | Defensive resilience | Low — single point of failure at lead |
  | Transit speed | Maximum — minimal coordination overhead |
  | Best used for | Long transits through uncontested space |

  ![Line formation](https://picsum.photos/id/534/800/300){.rounded-lg height="300" width="800"}
  :::

  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-chevrons-up" label="Wedge"}
  Concentrates offensive capability forward. The lead vessel provides sensor data while flanking vessels maintain overlapping weapons arcs.

  | Attribute | Rating |
  |-----------|--------|
  | Sensor coverage | Good — forward-focused with flanking overlap |
  | Offensive power | High — concentrated forward arcs |
  | Defensive resilience | Moderate — vulnerable from rear |
  | Transit speed | Moderate — requires formation keeping |
  | Best used for | Approach in contested environments |

  ![Wedge formation](https://picsum.photos/id/335/800/300){.rounded-lg height="300" width="800"}
  :::

  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-circle" label="Sphere"}
  All-around defensive coverage. Every approach vector is covered by at least two vessels.

  | Attribute | Rating |
  |-----------|--------|
  | Sensor coverage | Excellent — 360° coverage |
  | Offensive power | Distributed — no concentration of force |
  | Defensive resilience | Maximum — no blind spots |
  | Transit speed | Slow — high coordination overhead |
  | Best used for | Unknown threat axis, high-value asset protection |

  ![Sphere formation](https://picsum.photos/id/450/800/300){.rounded-lg height="300" width="800"}
  :::

  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-maximize" label="Dispersed Screen"}
  Maximizes patrol coverage. Vessels spread across a wide area with overlapping sensor zones but minimal weapons overlap.

  | Attribute | Rating |
  |-----------|--------|
  | Sensor coverage | Maximum — wide-area surveillance |
  | Offensive power | Very low — no mutual support |
  | Defensive resilience | Low — vessels isolated |
  | Transit speed | Varies per vessel |
  | Best used for | Area surveillance, early warning pickets |

  ![Dispersed screen](https://picsum.photos/id/120/800/300){.rounded-lg height="300" width="800"}
  :::

  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-shield" label="Close Escort"}
  Tightly clusters around a protected asset — a supply ship, a command vessel, or a damaged unit withdrawing from engagement.

  | Attribute | Rating |
  |-----------|--------|
  | Sensor coverage | Good — focused on protected unit |
  | Offensive power | Moderate — defensive orientation |
  | Defensive resilience | High for the protected asset |
  | Transit speed | Limited by slowest unit |
  | Best used for | Convoy escort, damaged unit withdrawal |

  ![Close escort](https://picsum.photos/id/137/800/300){.rounded-lg height="300" width="800"}
  :::
::

## Formation Transitions

Static formations are a liability in combat. The time required to transition — from Dispersed Screen to Wedge when a threat is identified, or from Wedge to Sphere when engaged from multiple vectors — can determine the outcome of an engagement.

::steps{level="3"}
### Threat detected or order given

AEGIS flags the triggering condition and recommends a target formation based on the tactical situation. The formation commander confirms or overrides.

### Optimal path calculation

The system calculates the shortest, safest movement path for each vessel to reach its new assigned position. Collision avoidance and sensor continuity are maintained throughout.

### Synchronized execution

All vessels begin maneuvering simultaneously on a shared timing signal. AEGIS monitors each vessel's progress and adjusts in real time if any unit falls behind or encounters obstacles.

### Formation integrity confirmed

AEGIS verifies that all vessels are in position and that sensor coverage, weapons arcs, and communications links meet the new formation's requirements. The transition is logged for after-action review.
::

The exercise report recommended fleet-wide adoption of automated formation management — a recommendation that has since been implemented across all major commands.
