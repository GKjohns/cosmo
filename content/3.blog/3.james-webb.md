## The Sensor Stack

::picture-and-text
### Layered Detection

Modern fleet operations depend on fusing data from multiple sensor types into a single coherent picture. No individual sensor provides complete awareness — radar excels at tracking solid objects at range, lidar offers precision mapping at closer distances, and passive EM listeners detect emissions without revealing the observer's position. AEGIS integrates all of these into a unified sensor overlay, weighted by confidence scores and cross-referenced for consistency.

### The Correlation Engine

Raw sensor data is noise. The AEGIS correlation engine transforms that noise into intelligence. By comparing returns across multiple sensor types and multiple vessels simultaneously, the engine can identify contacts that no single sensor would detect alone. A faint radar return that correlates with a thermal anomaly and a brief EM emission becomes a high-confidence contact — even when each individual reading falls below detection threshold.

## Fleet-Wide Sensor Networks

::picture-and-text
### Distributed Aperture

A single vessel's sensors are limited by its physical size. But a fleet operating in formation creates a distributed sensor aperture — effectively a telescope the size of the entire formation. AEGIS exploits this by synchronizing sensor timing across vessels and combining their returns interferometrically. The result is angular resolution orders of magnitude better than any single platform can achieve.

### Dealing with Degradation

Sensor systems fail. Battle damage, interference, power rationing during extended operations — all degrade the sensor picture. AEGIS tracks sensor health fleet-wide and automatically adjusts its fusion algorithms to compensate. When a vessel loses its primary radar, the system redistributes coverage responsibilities to neighboring vessels and adjusts confidence weightings accordingly. The tactical picture degrades gracefully rather than collapsing.

## Looking Ahead

::picture-and-text
### Next-Generation Arrays

The next generation of sensor arrays promises quantum-enhanced detection — using entangled photon pairs to detect objects at ranges currently impossible with classical sensors. AEGIS is being architected to support these new sensor types as they come online, with a modular sensor abstraction layer that allows new hardware to plug into the existing fusion pipeline without requiring system-wide updates.

### The Passive Advantage

There is growing doctrinal emphasis on passive sensing — detecting adversaries by their emissions rather than illuminating them with active sensors. Active radar reveals your position to anyone listening. AEGIS supports fully passive operational modes, building the tactical picture entirely from intercepted emissions, thermal signatures, and gravitational perturbations. In contested environments, the fleet that sees without being seen holds the decisive advantage.
