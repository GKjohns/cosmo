Modern fleet operations depend on fusing data from multiple sensor types into a single coherent picture. No individual sensor provides complete awareness. AEGIS integrates all of them into a unified overlay, weighted by confidence scores and cross-referenced for consistency.

## Supported Sensor Types

::card-group
  :::card
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-radar
  title: Phased Array Radar
  ---
  Active scanning for solid object detection and tracking at long range. Provides velocity, bearing, and range data. Reveals your position to anyone listening.
  :::

  :::card
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-scan-line
  title: Lidar Arrays
  ---
  Precision mapping and object classification at medium range. Excellent spatial resolution for formation-keeping and docking operations. Limited by particle density in debris fields.
  :::

  :::card
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-radio
  title: Passive EM Intercept
  ---
  Detects adversary emissions without revealing the observer. Provides signal classification, bearing estimation, and emitter identification. The foundation of stealth operations.
  :::

  :::card
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-thermometer
  title: Thermal Imaging
  ---
  Detects heat signatures from drives, reactors, and life support systems. Effective at all ranges where thermal contrast exists. Degraded by stellar proximity and nebula backgrounds.
  :::

  :::card
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-waves
  title: Gravitational Sensors
  ---
  Detects mass concentrations and gravitational anomalies. Essential for navigation hazard detection. Extremely long range but low resolution — confirms presence, not identity.
  :::

  :::card
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-satellite-dish
  title: Deep Space Telemetry
  ---
  Receives probe data from the DSTN relay network. Provides early warning of contacts beyond local sensor range. Latency varies from seconds to hours depending on relay distance.
  :::
::

## The Correlation Engine

Raw sensor data is noise. The AEGIS correlation engine transforms that noise into intelligence.

::note
A faint radar return that correlates with a thermal anomaly and a brief EM emission becomes a high-confidence contact — even when each individual reading falls below detection threshold independently.
::

The engine compares returns across multiple sensor types and multiple vessels simultaneously, identifying contacts that no single sensor would detect alone. This is where the distributed aperture concept becomes powerful.

## Fleet-Wide Sensor Networks

A single vessel's sensors are limited by its physical size. But a fleet operating in formation creates a distributed sensor aperture — effectively a telescope the size of the entire formation.

::callout
AEGIS exploits this by synchronizing sensor timing across vessels and combining their returns interferometrically. The result is angular resolution **orders of magnitude** better than any single platform can achieve.
::

## Dealing with Degradation

Sensor systems fail. Battle damage, interference, power rationing during extended operations — all degrade the sensor picture.

::tip
AEGIS tracks sensor health fleet-wide and automatically adjusts its fusion algorithms to compensate. When a vessel loses its primary radar, the system redistributes coverage responsibilities to neighboring vessels. The tactical picture degrades gracefully rather than collapsing.
::
