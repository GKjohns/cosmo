## The Speed of Light Problem

::picture-and-text
### Latency is the Enemy

At interplanetary distances, the speed of light becomes a tactical constraint. A signal from Earth to Mars takes between 4 and 24 minutes depending on orbital position. At interstellar distances, latency is measured in years. This fundamental physical limitation means that fleet operations cannot rely on centralized command-and-control — every vessel and every formation must be capable of autonomous operation when relay contact is lost.

### The Relay Network

AEGIS maintains a network of communication relay stations positioned at gravitationally stable points throughout known space. These relays amplify, route, and cache messages, providing store-and-forward capability when direct line-of-sight communication is impossible. The network is self-healing — when a relay goes offline, neighboring relays automatically adjust routing to maintain connectivity, and AEGIS command is notified of the gap in coverage.

## Encryption and Security

::picture-and-text
### Quantum Key Distribution

Fleet communications are encrypted using quantum key distribution (QKD), which provides information-theoretic security — meaning the encryption cannot be broken regardless of computational power. AEGIS manages key distribution across the fleet, rotating keys on schedule and providing emergency re-keying procedures when a vessel is compromised. The system tracks key freshness fleet-wide, ensuring no vessel is operating with expired cryptographic material.

### Emissions Control

In contested environments, the act of communicating can reveal your position. AEGIS supports multiple levels of emissions control (EMCON), from reduced transmission power to complete communications blackout. During EMCON, vessels rely on pre-arranged message schedules, burst transmissions, and directional tight-beam communications that are difficult for adversaries to intercept. AEGIS coordinates EMCON protocols fleet-wide, ensuring all vessels transition simultaneously.

## The Future of Fleet Comms

::picture-and-text
### Quantum Entanglement Channels

The next frontier in fleet communications is quantum entanglement — instantaneous information transfer regardless of distance. While current entanglement-based systems are limited in bandwidth, research is progressing rapidly. AEGIS is designed with a modular communications architecture that can incorporate entanglement channels as they become operationally viable, providing zero-latency command links that would fundamentally change fleet operations.

### Mesh Networking in Denied Environments

When relay infrastructure is destroyed or unavailable, fleets must create their own communications backbone. AEGIS supports ad-hoc mesh networking, where every vessel acts as both endpoint and relay. Messages route dynamically through the fleet, finding paths around damaged or jammed nodes. The mesh adapts continuously to formation changes and environmental conditions, maintaining connectivity even under the most challenging operational circumstances.
