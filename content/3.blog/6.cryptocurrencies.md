At interplanetary distances, the speed of light becomes a tactical constraint. At interstellar distances, latency is measured in years. This fundamental limitation means that fleet operations cannot rely on centralized command-and-control — every vessel must be capable of autonomous operation when relay contact is lost.

## Communication Technologies Compared

::tabs
  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-radio" label="RF/Microwave"}
  The workhorse of fleet communications. Radio frequency and microwave links provide reliable, well-understood communication at the cost of light-speed latency.

  | Parameter | Value |
  |-----------|-------|
  | Latency | Speed of light (minutes to hours at interplanetary range) |
  | Bandwidth | High (Gbps at short range, Kbps at interstellar) |
  | Detection risk | High — broadcasts in all directions |
  | Reliability | Excellent in clear space, degraded near stellar bodies |
  | Power draw | Moderate |

  RF remains the primary communication method for routine traffic. Its main disadvantage is detectability — any adversary within range can intercept omnidirectional RF broadcasts.
  :::

  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-focus" label="Tight-beam Laser"}
  Directional optical communication that's extremely difficult to intercept. The beam must be precisely aimed at the receiver, making eavesdropping nearly impossible.

  | Parameter | Value |
  |-----------|-------|
  | Latency | Speed of light |
  | Bandwidth | Very high (Tbps at short range) |
  | Detection risk | Very low — point-to-point only |
  | Reliability | Requires precise pointing; degraded by dust/debris |
  | Power draw | Low to moderate |

  Tight-beam is the preferred method for sensitive communications and EMCON operations. The tradeoff is that both sender and receiver must know each other's precise position.
  :::

  :::tabs-item{icon="i-lucide-atom" label="Quantum Entanglement"}
  Instantaneous information transfer regardless of distance. Currently experimental with severe bandwidth limitations.

  | Parameter | Value |
  |-----------|-------|
  | Latency | Zero (instantaneous) |
  | Bandwidth | Extremely low (bits per second) |
  | Detection risk | None — no signal traverses space |
  | Reliability | Dependent on entangled pair integrity |
  | Power draw | High (maintaining entanglement) |

  QE channels are reserved for priority command traffic only. A single entangled pair can transmit approximately 100 bits before decoherence. Pairs must be physically distributed in advance — you can't create new pairs at a distance.
  :::
::

## The Relay Network

AEGIS maintains a network of communication relay stations at gravitationally stable points throughout known space. These relays amplify, route, and cache messages.

::steps{level="3"}
### Message originates on vessel

The sender composes a message and selects priority level. AEGIS automatically chooses the optimal transmission method based on urgency, EMCON status, and available channels.

### Transmission to nearest relay

The message is encrypted and transmitted to the nearest relay station. If the relay is unavailable, AEGIS queues the message and routes through alternative paths — including mesh networking through nearby vessels.

### Relay network routing

Each relay examines the destination and forwards the message along the optimal path. Store-and-forward capability means messages survive temporary relay outages. The network is self-healing — when a relay goes offline, neighbors adjust routing automatically.

### Delivery to recipient

The destination relay transmits to the recipient vessel. Delivery confirmation propagates back through the network. If delivery fails, the message is cached at the nearest relay and retried on schedule.
::

## Emissions Control (EMCON)

::accordion
  :::accordion-item
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-volume-2
  label: "EMCON Level 1 — Normal Operations"
  ---
  All communication methods available. Standard RF broadcasts permitted. No restrictions on active sensor emissions. Used in uncontested space and friendly territory.
  :::

  :::accordion-item
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-volume-1
  label: "EMCON Level 2 — Reduced Emissions"
  ---
  RF power reduced to minimum effective range. Active sensor use limited to scheduled sweeps. Tight-beam preferred for all inter-vessel communication. Used when adversary detection is possible but not confirmed.
  :::

  :::accordion-item
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-volume-x
  label: "EMCON Level 3 — Communications Blackout"
  ---
  No RF or active sensor emissions permitted. All communication via tight-beam laser or physical messenger (shuttle). Vessels rely on pre-arranged message schedules and burst transmissions. Used in confirmed hostile environments.
  :::

  :::accordion-item
  ---
  icon: i-lucide-shield
  label: "EMCON Level 4 — Full Silent Running"
  ---
  All emissions suppressed including thermal management. Drive output minimized. Vessels operate on stored power and passive sensors only. Reserved for critical stealth operations where detection means mission failure.
  :::
::

## Mesh Networking in Denied Environments

When relay infrastructure is destroyed or unavailable, fleets create their own communications backbone. AEGIS supports ad-hoc mesh networking where every vessel acts as both endpoint and relay.

::tip
Messages route dynamically through the fleet, finding paths around damaged or jammed nodes. The mesh adapts continuously to formation changes and environmental conditions, maintaining connectivity even under the most challenging circumstances.
::

The mesh protocol is designed to be resilient against targeted jamming. If an adversary jams the link between two vessels, the mesh automatically routes through alternative paths — even if that means messages take a longer path through multiple intermediary vessels.
